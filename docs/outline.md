## Findings so far (for Gate A — proposed, not settled)

1. **The corpus is more mature than `concept.md` assumed.** It described the corpus
   as "dense, bullet-heavy, terse." In fact the Narrative & Game Design notes are
   substantial, well-cited, cross-linked syntheses — several already merged their own
   near-duplicates. Consequence: the note→article ratio is often **1:1 or 2:1**, not
   the heavy many-to-one merge the concept imagined. The guide's value-add is therefore
   *register* (naturalist head + scannable body) and *functional re-filing*, more than
   *compression*.
2. **The function-first spine is vindicated by the evidence.** The corpus is filed by
   medium (Interactive Fiction / Live / Tabletop / Puzzle Hunts); clustering by design
   function cuts across it cleanly. Within the calibration family alone, escape-room and
   scavenger notes redistribute to four different functional families (see Cross-family
   handoffs). This is the spine working as designed.
3. **Granularity signal:** the calibration family yields **14 articles in 5 groups** from
   ~25 claimed corpus notes (≈1.8 notes/article). If the other five families calibrate to
   this density, the guide lands at roughly **65–85 articles** — a medium-coarse field
   guide. Flagged for the human to accept or retune at Gate A.
5. **Taxonomy precedent (Gate A decision):** a comparative taxonomy becomes a *shallow
   router article* (the "map") plus *deep type articles*, never one squashed entry. Set
   when de-squashing Group 2B; fan-out agents should follow it wherever a note is a
   sprawling multi-type taxonomy.
4. **Puzzles is corpus-rich → few gaps.** This family is almost entirely `corpus`-backed.
   The gap list will be thin here; gaps should concentrate in under-covered families
   (tabletop and puzzle-hunt cross-pillar maps are unbuilt per the plan).

## Family 2: Puzzles, Clues & Information

*The cognitive loop of investigation and discovery: what the solver knows, the
structures they work against, the insight that resolves them, and the formats that
stage all three.*

### Group 2A — Information & Cluing (what the solver knows, and how fairly)

- **Information Architecture** — what each participant knows when, how it was acquired,
  what stays hidden and from whom. The fair-play tradition (Knox, Van Dine); the jigsaw,
  jubensha multi-act, epistemic-tier, and escape-room dependency-graph patterns.
  media: [live-game, puzzle-hunt, interactive-fiction]. provenance: corpus. *(hub)*
  - feeds: `information-architecture.md`
- **Cluing & Fairness** — every clue does work; fairness violations; the indirection
  gradient; clue versus gate. media: [puzzle-hunt, live-game, interactive-fiction].
  provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `cluing-principles.md` (cross-ref `mystery_conventions.md`, owned by another family)
- **Clue Sequencing & Chains** — ordering clues across a solve; the calibration opening;
  clue chains in hunts and trails. media: [live-game, puzzle-hunt]. provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `clue-sequencing.md`, `scavenger-and-treasure-hunts/clue-chain-design.md`

### Group 2B — Puzzle Mechanics (the structures themselves)

- **A Taxonomy of Puzzles** — the comparative map, deliberately a shallow router. Two
  complementary lenses: by **cognitive faculty** (word, logic, observation, physical,
  knowledge, meta) and by **experiential job in a live room** (search, observation,
  multi-step, parallel, meta, lateral, narrative-locked); the "atomic actions" idea; the
  anti-pattern of a room or hunt leaning on one category. Points to the deep articles
  below rather than deep-diving. media: [puzzle-hunt, live-game, tabletop, interactive-fiction].
  provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `puzzle-types.md` (split), `escape-room-design/puzzle-taxonomy.md` (split)
- **Search, Observation & Lateral Thinking** — the "noticing and world-affordance" puzzle
  types that the mechanism articles below do not cover: search, observation, lateral-thinking,
  narrative-locked, and physical-manipulation puzzles, with their shine/fail conditions.
  media: [live-game, puzzle-hunt]. provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `escape-room-design/puzzle-taxonomy.md` (split), `puzzle-types.md` (split)
- **Ciphers & Codes** — selecting and fairly deploying ciphers and encodings.
  media: [puzzle-hunt, live-game]. provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `cipher-selection.md`
- **Extraction, Indexing & Meta-Puzzles** — answers-as-data; indexing; the meta as the
  structural innovation of the hunt; multi-step construction. media: [puzzle-hunt].
  provenance: corpus.
  - feeds: `meta-puzzles.md`, `multistep-puzzles.md`
- **Self-Resolving Puzzles** — the mechanism as judge; the false-positive failure mode;
  reset discipline. media: [live-game]. provenance: corpus. *(hub; cross-ref Space &
  Materiality "Digital Props")*
  - feeds: `self-resolving-puzzles.md`

mystery-cluing IF note, a physical-clue-format prop note, a puzzle-accessibility note):
those arrive as `route_out → Puzzles` flags and are folded into this family at reconcile
