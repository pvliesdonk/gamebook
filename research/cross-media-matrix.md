## Boardgame integration finding

The boardgame-specific articles in the Systems & Mechanics family (the `boardgame-design` /
`boardgame-genres` corpus clusters) split cleanly into two halves under cross-media analysis.

**Isolated — Tabletop-only (● Tabletop, – everywhere else):** marks `IF – · Live – · Tabletop ● · Puzzle-Hunt –`

- **Boardgame Mechanism Vocabulary**
- **Euro vs Ameritrash: The Two Design Poles**
- **Deckbuilders**

These three are genre / component-vocabulary catalogues. No corpus note outside the boardgame
clusters touches worker-placement / engine-building / deck-as-strategic-object, and the notes'
own See-Also sections are mutually self-referential. They are a self-contained Tabletop island.

**Cross-connecting — boardgame *design principles* that manifest in other media** (all are `Tabletop ●`):

- **Decision Space & Choice Design** — `IF ◐ · Live ◐ · PH –` → IF (meaningful-choice craft) + Live (puzzle-taxonomy)
- **Theme-Mechanism Integration** — `IF ◐ · Live ◐ · PH –` → IF (ludonarrative dissonance) + Live (diegetic mechanics)
- **Elegance, Emergence & Randomness** — `IF ◐ · Live – · PH –` → IF (`mechanics_design_patterns`)
- **Replayability** — `IF ◐ · Live ◐ · PH –` → IF (Rashomon / branching) + Live (escape-room branching)
- **Cooperative & Legacy Genres** — `IF – · Live ◐ · PH –` → Live (cooperative-vs-adversarial formats)
- **Social Performance Genres** — `IF – · Live ◐ · PH –` → Live (social-deduction / trust-and-social-pressure)
- **Solo Modes** — `IF ◐ · Live – · PH –` → IF (oracle / LLM-as-opponent, weak)

These earn cross-media placement: their notes already cite the IF `mechanics_design_patterns`
and `branching_narrative_craft` notes, and the Live escape-room and group-dynamics clusters,
in their See-Also sections.

**Recommendation — RESCOPE (not cut).** The boardgame *principles* (Decision Space,
Theme-Mechanism Integration, Elegance/Emergence, Replayability, Solo Modes, and the two
social / co-op genre articles) are well-integrated and should be kept. The three *catalogue*
articles (Boardgame Mechanism Vocabulary, Euro vs Ameritrash, Deckbuilders) are genuinely
isolated from the other three media and are the candidates for cut, merge into a single
reference appendix, or explicit "Tabletop-only" scoping.

**Gate C1 decision (applied):** RESCOPE. The 3 catalogue articles are kept but demoted to a
**Tabletop-only reference appendix** (outline `Appendix 6Z`); the 7 boardgame design-principle
articles stay as cross-media articles. No corpus notes moved; article count unchanged (116 =
113 cross-media + 3 appendix). See `docs/outline.md` Family 6.
